## MCQ 1-10: Basic Charge Properties

**Q1.** Two charges of +2 μC and -3 μC are placed in vacuum. The nature of force between them is:
- (a) Attractive
- (b) Repulsive
- (c) No force
- (d) None of these

> [!ANSWER] (a) Attractive
> Unlike charges attract. Coulomb force is attractive between opposite charges.

**Q2.** The value of elementary charge e is:
- (a) 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
- (b) 9.1 × 10⁻³¹ kg
- (c) 6.6 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
- (d) 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ F

> [!ANSWER] (a) 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
> e = 1.602192 × 10⁻¹⁹ C is the magnitude of charge on electron/proton.

**Q3.** Quantisation of charge means:
- (a) Charge can be created
- (b) Charge is continuous
- (c) Charge exists as integer multiples of e
- (d) Charge is conserved

> [!ANSWER] (c) Charge exists as integer multiples of e
> q = ne where n = 0, ±1, ±2, ... Charge is discrete, not continuous.

**Q4.** Which of the following is conserved?
- (a) Mass only
- (b) Charge only
- (c) Both mass and charge
- (d) Neither mass nor charge

> [!ANSWER] (c) Both mass and charge
> Charge conservation is a fundamental law; mass-energy is also conserved (including in nuclear reactions).

**Q5.** When glass is rubbed with silk:
- (a) Glass gains protons
- (b) Glass loses electrons
- (c) Silk gains electrons
- (d) Both (b) and (c)

> [!ANSWER] (d) Both (b) and (c)
> Electrons transfer from glass to silk. Glass loses electrons (positive), silk gains electrons (negative).

**Q6.** One coulomb is approximately equal to:
- (a) 6.25 × 10¹⁸ electrons
- (b) 6.25 × 10¹⁹ electrons
- (c) 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ electrons
- (d) None

> [!ANSWER] (a) 6.25 × 10¹⁸ electrons
> n = Q/e = 1/(1.6×10⁻¹⁹) = 6.25×10¹⁸ electrons

**Q7.** The force between two charges is F. If distance is doubled, force becomes:
- (a) F/4
- (b) F/2
- (c) 2F
- (d) 4F

> [!ANSWER] (a) F/4
> F ∝ 1/r². When r → 2r, F → F/4.

**Q8.** A body is negatively charged because:
- (a) It has lost protons
- (b) It has gained electrons
- (c) It has lost neutrons
- (d) It has gained protons

> [!ANSWER] (b) It has gained electrons
> Negative charge means excess of electrons over protons.

**Q9.** The ratio Fₑ/Fɢ between an electron and proton at same distance is approximately:
- (a) 2.4 × 10³⁹
- (b) 9.1 × 10⁻³¹
- (c) 1.8 × 10¹⁸
- (d) 10⁴³

> [!ANSWER] (a) 2.4 × 10³⁹
> Fₑ/Fɢ = ke²/(Gmₑmₚ) ≈ 2.4 × 10³⁹. Electric force dominates at atomic scales.

**Q10.** The additivity of charge means:
- (a) Charges add like vectors
- (b) Charges add like scalars
- (c) Charge has direction
- (d) Charge varies with position

> [!ANSWER] (b) Charges add like scalars
> Total charge = algebraic sum of individual charges. No direction involved (unlike force).

## MCQ 11-25: Coulomb's Law

**Q11.** The SI unit of ε₀ is:
- (a) C²/N·m²
- (b) N·m²/C²
- (c) C/N·m²
- (d) C²·N/m²

> [!ANSWER] (a) C²/N·m²
> ε₀ = 8.854 × 10⁻¹² C²/N·m² (permittivity of free space)

**Q12.** Coulomb's constant k equals:
- (a) ε₀/4π
- (b) 1/(4πε₀)
- (c) 4π/ε₀
- (d) ε₀/4π²

> [!ANSWER] (b) 1/(4πε₀)
> k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C² = 1/(4π × 8.85×10⁻¹²)

**Q13.** Two charges of +1 C each are placed 1 m apart. Force between them is:
- (a) 9 × 10⁹ N
- (b) 8 × 10⁹ N
- (c) 9 × 10⁶ N
- (d) 9 × 10³ N

> [!ANSWER] (a) 9 × 10⁹ N
> F = k × 1 × 1/1² = 9 × 10⁹ N

**Q14.** The force between two charges is F. If each charge is doubled and distance halved, new force is:
- (a) 4F
- (b) 8F
- (c) 16F
- (d) 32F

> [!ANSWER] (c) 16F
> F ∝ q₁q₂/r². New: (2×2)/(0.5)² = 4/0.25 = 16 times.

**Q15.** Coulomb's law is valid for:
- (a) Point charges only
- (b) Only when charges are stationary
- (c) Any charge distribution
- (d) At atomic distances only

> [!ANSWER] (b) Only when charges are stationary
> Coulomb's law as stated is for static charges. Moving charges involve additional magnetic effects.

**Q16.** The constant 1/(4πε₀) in SI units has value:
- (a) 9 × 10⁹
- (b) 8.85 × 10⁻¹²
- (c) 4π × 10⁻⁷
- (d) 10⁻⁹

> [!ANSWER] (a) 9 × 10⁹
> k = 9 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²

**Q17.** The electrostatic force is stronger than gravitational force by factor:
- (a) 10³⁶
- (b) 10³⁹
- (c) 10⁴²
- (d) 10⁴⁵

> [!ANSWER] (b) 10³⁹
> Fₑ/Fɢ ≈ 2.4 × 10³⁹ for electron-proton system.

**Q18.** If dielectric constant of medium is K, the force between charges becomes:
- (a) K times
- (b) 1/K times
- (c) Same
- (d) K² times

> [!ANSWER] (b) 1/K times
> F = (1/4πε₀K)(q₁q₂/r²). Force decreases in medium.

**Q19.** The dimensional formula of Coulomb's constant k is:
- (a) ML³T⁻⁴A⁻²
- (b) MLT⁻⁴A²
- (c) M⁻¹L³T⁴A⁻²
- (d) ML²T⁻²A⁻²

> [!ANSWER] (a) ML³T⁻⁴A⁻²
> k = F×r²/q² = (MLT⁻²)(L²)/(A²T²) = ML³T⁻⁴A⁻²

**Q20.** For static charges, the net force on a charge due to multiple charges is:
- (a) Arithmetic sum
- (b) Geometric sum
- (c) Vector sum
- (d) Scalar sum

> [!ANSWER] (c) Vector sum
> Forces are vectors. Must use superposition principle.

## MCQ 21-40: Electric Field

**Q21.** Electric field intensity E is defined as:
- (a) F × q
- (b) F/q
- (c) q/F
- (d) q²/F

> [!ANSWER] (b) F/q
> E = F/q = force per unit positive charge.

**Q22.** The unit of electric field is:
- (a) N/C or V/m
- (b) C/N
- (c) V·C
- (d) J/C

> [!ANSWER] (a) N/C or V/m
> E = F/q has units N/C, which equals V/m (since V = J/C).

**Q23.** Electric field at a point due to a positive charge is:
- (a) Directed toward the charge
- (b) Directed away from the charge
- (c) Perpendicular to the charge
- (d) Zero

> [!ANSWER] (b) Directed away from the charge
> Field lines emanate outward from positive charges.

**Q24.** The electric field inside a uniformly charged spherical shell is:
- (a) kQ/R²
- (b) kQ/r²
- (c) Zero
- (d) Infinite

> [!ANSWER] (c) Zero
> By Gauss's law, no enclosed charge → zero field inside.

**Q25.** The electric field at distance r from point charge Q is E. If charge is doubled and distance halved, field becomes:
- (a) 2E
- (b) 4E
- (c) 8E
- (d) 16E

> [!ANSWER] (c) 8E
> E ∝ Q/r². New: (2)/(0.5)² = 2/0.25 = 8 times.

**Q26.** Electric field is a:
- (a) Scalar quantity
- (b) Vector quantity
- (c) Neither scalar nor vector
- (d) Dimensionless quantity

> [!ANSWER] (b) Vector quantity
> Electric field has both magnitude and direction.

**Q27.** At a point where electric field is zero:
- (a) Potential is also zero
- (b) Potential may or may not be zero
- (c) Potential is maximum
- (d) Charge placed there explodes

> [!ANSWER] (b) Potential may or may not be zero
> Zero field doesn't imply zero potential. E = -dV/dr; V can have extremum where E = 0.

**Q28.** The magnitude of electric field at the surface of a proton is about:
- (a) 10⁶ N/C
- (b) 10¹² N/C
- (c) 10⁸ N/C
- (d) 10⁴ N/C

> [!ANSWER] (b) 10¹² N/C
> E = ke/R² ≈ 9×10⁹ × 1.6×10⁻¹⁹/(10⁻¹⁵)² ≈ 10¹² N/C

**Q29.** The electric field 5 cm from a charge is 10⁶ N/C. The charge is:
- (a) 2.8 × 10⁻⁶ C
- (b) 2.8 × 10⁻⁷ C
- (c) 2.8 × 10⁻⁸ C
- (d) 2.8 × 10⁻⁹ C

> [!ANSWER] (a) 2.8 × 10⁻⁶ C
> Q = Er²/k = 10⁶ × (0.05)²/(9×10⁹) = 2.78 × 10⁻⁷ C. Wait: 10⁶×2.5×10⁻³/9×10⁹ = 2.78×10⁻⁷ C. Option (b).

**Q30.** An electron is placed in uniform electric field. It will:
- (a) Move perpendicular to field
- (b) Move along the field
- (c) Not move
- (d) Move opposite to field

> [!ANSWER] (d) Move opposite to field
> Negative charge experiences force opposite to field direction.

**Q31.** Two point charges +q and +q are placed at distance d. The electric field at midpoint is:
- (a) Zero
- (b) kq/d²
- (c) 2kq/d²
- (d) 4kq/d²

> [!ANSWER] (a) Zero
> Fields from both charges are equal and opposite at midpoint.

**Q32.** The field due to a dipole at distance r on the axial line is:
- (a) kp/r³
- (b) 2kp/r³
- (c) kp/r²
- (d) 2kp/r²

> [!ANSWER] (b) 2kp/r³
> E_axial = 2p/(4πε₀r³) = 2kp/r³ where k = 1/4πε₀

**Q33.** Electric field at a point is maximum when:
- (a) tanθ = 1
- (b) sinθ = 1
- (c) cosθ = 1
- (d) θ = 45°

> [!ANSWER] None - For dipole: E = (2p cosθ)/r³ on axis; maximum at θ = 0°.

**Q34.** A ring of radius R with charge Q uniformly distributed. The electric field on its axis is:
- (a) kQ/R²
- (b) kQ/(R²+x²)
- (c) kQx/(R²+x²)^(3/2)
- (d) kQ/(R²+x²)^(1/2)

> [!ANSWER] (c) kQx/(R²+x²)^(3/2)
> Standard result for ring on axis.

**Q35.** The electric field inside a solid conducting sphere with uniform charge is:
- (a) Constant
- (b) Zero
- (c) Varies as 1/r
- (d) Varies as 1/r²

> [!ANSWER] (a) Constant
> Inside uniformly charged solid sphere, E = (ρr)/(3ε₀) which varies linearly with r.

## MCQ 36-55: Gauss's Law & Applications

**Q36.** Gauss's law is valid for:
- (a) Any closed surface
- (b) Only spherical surfaces
- (c) Only cubical surfaces
- (d) Only symmetric surfaces

> [!ANSWER] (a) Any closed surface
> Gauss's law is general and valid for any closed surface shape.

**Q37.** Electric flux is:
- (a) Vector
- (b) Scalar
- (c) Neither
- (d) Dimensionless

> [!ANSWER] (b) Scalar
> Flux = E·S = ES cosθ, which is a scalar (dot product of vectors).

**Q38.** The SI unit of electric flux is:
- (a) N·m²
- (b) N·m²/C
- (c) V·m
- (d) All of these

> [!ANSWER] (d) All of these
> N·m²/C = V·m (since V = J/C = N·m/C)

**Q39.** The flux through a closed surface depends on:
- (a) Shape of surface
- (b) Position of charges inside
- (c) Charge enclosed only
- (d) All charges inside and outside

> [!ANSWER] (c) Charge enclosed only
> Gauss's law: Φ = q_enclosed/ε₀. Shape and external charges don't affect net flux.

**Q40.** Electric field due to infinite line charge is proportional to:
- (a) 1/r
- (b) 1/r²
- (c) r
- (d) r²

> [!ANSWER] (a) 1/r
> E = λ/(2πε₀r) ∝ 1/r for infinite line.

**Q41.** Electric field due to infinite plane sheet is:
- (a) σ/ε₀
- (b) σ/(2ε₀)
- (c) σ/(4ε₀)
- (d) 2σ/ε₀

> [!ANSWER] (b) σ/(2ε₀)
> Field from each infinite sheet is σ/(2ε₀), independent of distance.

**Q42.** The field outside a charged spherical conductor is:
- (a) Less than surface field
- (b) Equal to surface field
- (c) More than surface field
- (d) Zero

> [!ANSWER] (b) Equal to surface field
> For spherical conductor, field outside = kQ/r², at surface = kQ/R². Different values unless r = R.

**Q43.** Two parallel infinite sheets have surface densities +σ and -σ. The field between them is:
- (a) Zero
- (b) σ/ε₀
- (c) σ/(2ε₀)
- (d) 2σ/ε₀

> [!ANSWER] (b) σ/ε₀
> Between unlike sheets: E = σ/(2ε₀) + σ/(2ε₀) = σ/ε₀

**Q44.** A charge q is placed at center of sphere. Flux through hemisphere is:
- (a) q/ε₀
- (b) q/(2ε₀)
- (c) q/(4ε₀)
- (d) q/(8ε₀)

> [!ANSWER] (b) q/(2ε₀)
> Total flux = q/ε₀. By symmetry, half through each hemisphere.

**Q45.** For a cylindrical Gaussian surface around a wire, flux through ends is:
- (a) E × πr²
- (b) Zero
- (c) 2Eπrl
- (d) E × 2πrl

> [!ANSWER] (b) Zero
> E is radial, perpendicular to ends. E·dS = 0 at ends.

**Q46.** The Gaussian surface cannot pass through:
- (a) Continuous charge distribution
- (b) Point charges
- (c) Both
- (d) None

> [!ANSWER] (b) Point charges
> Field is undefined at point charges (becomes infinite). Gaussian surface can pass through continuous distributions.

**Q47.** The field inside a uniformly charged solid sphere varies:
- (a) As 1/r
- (b) As 1/r²
- (c) Linearly with r
- (d) Constant

> [!ANSWER] (c) Linearly with r
> E = (ρr)/(3ε₀) ∝ r inside solid sphere.

**Q48.** The potential inside a conductor is:
- (a) Variable
- (b) Constant
- (c) Zero
- (d) Infinite

> [!ANSWER] (b) Constant
> E = -dV/dr = 0 inside conductor, so V is constant throughout.

**Q49.** A charge is placed outside a closed surface. The flux through surface is:
- (a) Positive
- (b) Negative
- (c) Zero
- (d) Cannot say

> [!ANSWER] (c) Zero
> Net flux depends only on enclosed charge. External charge doesn't contribute to net flux.

**Q50.** For a point charge at the center of a cube, flux through one face is:
- (a) q/ε₀
- (b) q/(6ε₀)
- (c) q/(4πε₀)
- (d) qε₀

> [!ANSWER] (b) q/(6ε₀)
> Total flux = q/ε₀. Six equal faces → each gets q/(6ε₀)

## MCQ 51-70: Electric Dipole

**Q51.** The dipole moment of a dipole is:
- (a) qa
- (b) 2qa
- (c) q/a
- (d) q²a

> [!ANSWER] (b) 2qa
> p = q × 2a (charge × separation), where 2a is distance between charges.

**Q52.** SI unit of dipole moment is:
- (a) C·m⁻¹
- (b) C·m
- (c) C/m²
- (d) C²/m

> [!ANSWER] (b) C·m
> Dipole moment p = q × d, giving unit C·m.

**Q53.** The torque on a dipole in uniform electric field is:
- (a) pE sinθ
- (b) pE cosθ
- (c) pE tanθ
- (d) pE

> [!ANSWER] (a) pE sinθ
> τ = pE sinθ = |p × E|, maximum when perpendicular.

**Q54.** The potential energy of a dipole in uniform field is minimum when angle between p and E is:
- (a) 0°
- (b) 90°
- (c) 180°
- (d) 45°

> [!ANSWER] (a) 0°
> U = -pE cosθ, minimum when cosθ = 1 (θ = 0°), i.e., aligned.

**Q55.** The field due to a dipole at large distance varies as:
- (a) 1/r
- (b) 1/r²
- (c) 1/r³
- (d) 1/r⁴

> [!ANSWER] (c) 1/r³
> Unlike 1/r² for point charge, dipole field falls off faster: E ∝ 1/r³.

**Q56.** A dipole is placed in uniform field. The net force on it is:
- (a) pE
- (b) pE sinθ
- (c) Zero
- (d) pE cosθ

> [!ANSWER] (c) Zero
> Forces on +q and -q are equal and opposite, cancel out.

**Q57.** The ratio of field on axis to field on equatorial plane of dipole is:
- (a) 1
- (b) 2
- (c) 1/2
- (d) 4

> [!ANSWER] (b) 2
> E_axis = 2p/(4πε₀r³), E_equatorial = p/(4πε₀r³). Ratio = 2.

**Q58.** CO₂ molecule has dipole moment:
- (a) Zero
- (b) Non-zero
- (c) Infinite
- (d) Cannot say

> [!ANSWER] (a) Zero
> CO₂ is linear and symmetric, centers of + and - charges coincide.

**Q59.** H₂O molecule has dipole moment:
- (a) Zero
- (b) Non-zero
- (c) Very small
- (d) Very large

> [!ANSWER] (b) Non-zero
> H₂O is bent shape, electronegativity difference creates separation.

**Q60.** A dipole starts from θ = 90° and reaches θ = 0°. Work done is:
- (a) pE
- (b) 2pE
- (c) pE/2
- (d) Zero

> [!ANSWER] (a) pE
> W = U_final - U_initial = (-pE cos0°) - (-pE cos90°) = -pE + 0 = -pE. But magnitude = pE.

## MCQ 61-80: Advanced & Mixed

**Q61.** The number of field lines starting from +q and ending at -q is:
- (a) Proportional to q
- (b) Proportional to q²
- (c) Independent of q
- (d) Zero

> [!ANSWER] (a) Proportional to q
> Number of field lines drawn is proportional to magnitude of charge.

**Q62.** Two field lines:
- (a) Can cross
- (b) Cannot cross
- (c) Merge at infinity
- (d) None

> [!ANSWER] (b) Cannot cross
> At intersection, field would have two directions → impossible.

**Q63.** Field lines are always perpendicular to:
- (a) Equipotential surfaces
- (b) Electric dipole axis
- (c) Gaussian surfaces
- (d) Point charges

> [!ANSWER] (a) Equipotential surfaces
> E = -dV/dr; along equipotential, dV = 0, so E ⟂ surface.

**Q64.** The work done in moving charge around a closed loop in electric field is:
- (a) qE × 2πr
- (b) Zero
- (c) qE
- (d) Infinite

> [!ANSWER] (b) Zero
> Electrostatic field is conservative; work in closed loop = 0.

**Q65.** When a dielectric is placed in electric field, the field inside:
- (a) Increases
- (b) Decreases
- (c) Unchanged
- (d) Becomes zero

> [!ANSWER] (b) Decreases
> Dielectric reduces effective field: E = E₀/K, where K > 1.

**Q66.** The polarizability of H₂O is:
- (a) Zero
- (b) Small
- (c) Large
- (d) Infinite

> [!ANSWER] (c) Large
> H₂O has permanent dipole moment and high polarizability.

**Q67.** The concept of electric field lines was introduced by:
- (a) Coulomb
- (b) Faraday
- (c) Maxwell
- (d) Gauss

> [!ANSWER] (b) Faraday
> Michael Faraday introduced field lines as a visualization tool.

**Q68.** Which is not a property of electric field lines?
- (a) Start from + charge
- (b) End at - charge
- (c) Can form closed loops
- (d) Never cross

> [!ANSWER] (c) Can form closed loops
> Electrostatic field lines cannot form closed loops (conservative field).

**Q69.** The torque on a dipole is maximum when angle between p and E is:
- (a) 0°
- (b) 45°
- (c) 90°
- (d) 180°

> [!ANSWER] (c) 90°
> τ = pE sinθ; maximum when sinθ = 1, i.e., θ = 90°.

**Q70.** In a uniform field, if a dipole is rotated from alignment to anti-alignment, work done is:
- (a) pE
- (b) 2pE
- (c) 4pE
- (d) Zero

> [!ANSWER] (b) 2pE
> W = U₂ - U₁ = (+pE) - (-pE) = 2pE

## MCQ 71-85: Numerical & Mixed

**Q71.** A charge of 10 μC produces electric field 10⁶ N/C at distance r. Value of r is:
- (a) 0.03 m
- (b) 0.3 m
- (c) 3 m
- (d) 30 m

> [!ANSWER] (b) 0.3 m
> r = √(kQ/E) = √(9×10⁹ × 10×10⁻⁶ / 10⁶) = √(90) = 9.5 m. Wait: √(9×10⁹×10⁻⁵/10⁶) = √(9×10⁻²) = 0.3 m.

**Q72.** An electron moving with velocity 10⁶ m/s enters field 10⁴ N/C. Distance traveled before stopping is (neglecting gravity):
- (a) 2.8 mm
- (b) 2.8 cm
- (c) 28 cm
- (d) 2.8 m

> [!ANSWER] (a) 2.8 mm
> s = mv²/(2eE) = (9.1×10⁻³¹ × 10¹²)/(2 × 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ × 10⁴) ≈ 2.84 × 10⁻³ m

**Q73.** Flux through sphere with point charge q at center is Φ. If charge is doubled, flux becomes:
- (a) Φ/2
- (b) Φ
- (c) 2Φ
- (d) 4Φ

> [!ANSWER] (c) 2Φ
> Φ = q/ε₀, so doubling q doubles flux.

**Q74.** Two charges +q and -q form a dipole. Net charge is:
- (a) q
- (b) -q
- (c) Zero
- (d) 2q

> [!ANSWER] (c) Zero
> +q + (-q) = 0. Dipole has zero net charge.

**Q75.** A dipole has moment p and is placed in field E. Maximum torque is:
- (a) pE
- (b) pE/2
- (c) 2pE
- (d) Zero

> [!ANSWER] (a) pE
> τ_max = pE sin90° = pE

## MCQ 76-100: JEE Advanced Patterns

**Q76.** [JEE Pattern] A charge +q is brought towards a neutral conducting sphere. The field inside the sphere is:
- (a) Inwards
- (b) Outwards
- (c) Zero
- (d) Cannot determine

> [!ANSWER] (c) Zero
> By induction, charge redistributes on outer surface, leaving interior field zero.

**Q77.** [JEE Pattern] A charge is placed at corner of a cube. Flux through opposite face is:
- (a) q/(6ε₀)
- (b) q/(12ε₀)
- (c) q/(24ε₀)
- (d) q/(2ε₀)

> [!ANSWER] (c) q/(24ε₀)
> Complete cube would have q/ε₀. This face is 1/6 of 1/4 = q/(24ε₀)

**Q78.** [JEE Pattern] Three charges at vertices of equilateral triangle. At centroid, field is:
- (a) Always zero
- (b) Zero if equal charges
- (c) Non-zero always
- (d) Infinite

> [!ANSWER] (b) Zero if equal charges
> By symmetry, fields cancel for equal charges. For unequal, net field is non-zero.

**Q79.** [JEE Pattern] A particle of charge q and mass m is projected towards a fixed charge Q with velocity v. Minimum distance of approach is:
- (a) kqQ/(½mv²)
- (b) kqQ/(mv²)
- (c) 2kqQ/(mv²)
- (d) 4kqQ/(mv²)

> [!ANSWER] (c) 2kqQ/(mv²)
> K.E. = ½mv² = P.E. = kqQ/r_min, solving gives r_min = 2kqQ/(mv²)

**Q80.** [JEE Pattern] Work done in rotating dipole from 0° to 180° is:
- (a) 2pE
- (b) -2pE
- (c) pE
- (d) Zero

> [!ANSWER] (a) 2pE
> W = U(180°) - U(0°) = (+pE) - (-pE) = 2pE

**Q81.** [JEE Pattern] Two charges +q and +4q are at distance d. Point where field is zero is at distance from +q:
- (a) d/3
- (b) d/2
- (c) 2d/3
- (d) d/4

> [!ANSWER] (a) d/3
> For unlike charges, point is between them. Let x from +q: kq/x² = k4q/(d-x)² → x = d/3.

**Q82.** [JEE Pattern] A dipole is placed in non-uniform field. It experiences:
- (a) Torque only
- (b) Force only
- (c) Both torque and force
- (d) No force or torque

> [!ANSWER] (c) Both torque and force
> In non-uniform field, forces on +q and -q are unequal → net force AND torque.

**Q83.** [JEE Pattern] The angular frequency of oscillation of a dipole in uniform field is:
- (a) √(pE/I)
- (b) √(pE/I)
- (c) √(qE/m)
- (d) √(kq²/mr³)

> [!ANSWER] (a) √(pE/I)
> Restoring torque = pE sinθ ≈ pEθ, Iα = pEθ → ω² = pE/I

**Q84.** [JEE Pattern] A charge is placed at center of spherical cavity inside conducting sphere. Field outside is:
- (a) Zero
- (b) As if all charge at center
- (c) kQ/r²
- (d) Cannot say

> [!ANSWER] (c) kQ/r²
> Conductor shields interior but field outside depends on total induced charge.

**Q85.** [JEE Pattern] Potential at center of ring of radius R with charge Q is:
- (a) kQ/R
- (b) kQ/R²
- (c) kQR
- (d) kQ/R³

> [!ANSWER] (a) kQ/R
> V = kQ/r for any point at distance r from point charge. For ring, all charge at distance R.

**Q86.** [JEE Pattern] Two spheres of radii r and 2r have same charge density. Ratio of their fields at surface is:
- (a) 1:1
- (b) 1:2
- (c) 2:1
- (d) 1:4

> [!ANSWER] (c) 2:1
> E = σ/ε₀ = ρr/3ε₀. For same ρ, E ∝ r. Ratio = 2r:r = 2:1.

**Q87.** [JEE Pattern] A semi-circular ring has charge Q. Field at center is:

**Q88.** [JEE Pattern] The field lines near a point charge are:
- (a) Parallel

- (c) Curved
- (d) Circular

> [!ANSWER] (b) Radial

**Q89.** [JEE Pattern] Four charges at corners of square. Force on any charge is proportional to:
- (a) q²/r²
- (b) √2 q²/r²
- (c) (1+√2)q²/r²
- (d) 2q²/r²

> [!ANSWER] (c) (1+√2)q²/r²
> F_adjacent = kq²/a², F_diagonal = kq²/(a√2)² = kq²/a². Vector sum: F = F₁ + F₂ = kq²/a²(1 + 1/√2) = kq²/a²(1+√2)/√2 = (1+√2)q²/r² where r=a

**Q90.** [JEE Pattern] A charge +q is placed at (a,0) and -q at (-a,0). At (0,a), potential is:

**Q91.** [JEE Pattern] Flux linked with area vector A in uniform field E is:

**Q92.** [JEE Pattern] The electric field on the axis of a charged disc at center is:

**Q95.** [JEE Pattern] Two charges q and -q separated by distance d form a dipole. If d is halved:
