# Gauss Law

Gauss Law relates total electric flux through a closed surface to the charge enclosed by that surface.

$$\oint \vec{E}\cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0}$$

Use it with [[Electric Field]] and [[Electrostatics Core Map]] when symmetry makes the field magnitude constant on the surface.

## Symmetry Checklist

- Spherical symmetry: point charge, uniformly charged shell.
- Cylindrical symmetry: infinite line charge.
- Planar symmetry: infinite sheet charge.

Common mistake: using total charge near the surface instead of enclosed charge.

- Q:: What does Gauss Law actually count? A:: Net charge enclosed by the chosen closed surface.
- Q:: Why can flux be zero while electric field is not zero? A:: Equal inward and outward flux can cancel when no net charge is enclosed.
- MCQ:: For an infinite sheet with surface charge density sigma, field magnitude is: Options:: sigma/epsilon0 | sigma/(2epsilon0) | 2sigma/epsilon0 | zero Answer:: sigma/(2epsilon0)
