## QUIZ 1: TRUE/FALSE (1-15)

**Q1.** Electric charge is quantised.
→ **TRUE** (q = ne)

**Q2.** Like charges repel each other.
→ **TRUE**

**Q3.** Charge can be created but not destroyed.
→ **FALSE** (Charge is conserved - cannot be created or destroyed)

**Q4.** The SI unit of electric field is N/C or V/m.
→ **TRUE**

**Q5.** Electric field inside a conductor is zero in electrostatic equilibrium.
→ **TRUE**

**Q6.** Two field lines can cross each other.
→ **FALSE**

**Q7.** Electric field due to dipole varies as 1/r².
→ **FALSE** (It varies as 1/r³)

**Q8.** Gauss's law is valid for any shape of closed surface.
→ **TRUE**

**Q9.** The dipole moment vector points from positive to negative charge.
→ **FALSE** (It points from negative to positive charge)

**Q10.** Work done in moving charge in closed loop in electrostatic field is zero.
→ **TRUE**

**Q11.** Electric field is a scalar quantity.
→ **FALSE** (It's a vector)

**Q12.** Coulomb's force is stronger than gravitational force by factor 10³⁹.
→ **TRUE**

**Q13.** Potential at center of ring is maximum.
→ **FALSE** (Potential is minimum, actually same everywhere on ring)

**Q14.** Flux through closed surface depends on shape of surface.
→ **FALSE** (Only on enclosed charge)

**Q15.** A dipole in uniform field experiences torque but no net force.
→ **TRUE**

**Q26.** Electron has _____ charge compared to proton.
→ **equal magnitude, opposite sign**

**Q27.** Field outside charged sphere = k × _____/r².
→ **Q** (total charge)

**Q28.** Dipole field varies as 1/_____.
→ **r³**

**Q29.** Charge acquired by glass rod rubbed with silk is _____.

## QUIZ 5: ASSERTION-REASON (51-60)

**Q51.** Assertion: Charge is quantized.
Reason: Charge exists only as integer multiples of e.
→ **Both A and R are true; R explains A.**

---

**Q52.** Assertion: Electric field inside spherical shell is zero.
Reason: No charge enclosed inside shell.
→ **Both true, but reason is incomplete. Zero field is due to spherical symmetry AND no enclosed charge.**

---

**Q53.** Assertion: Two field lines cannot cross.
Reason: At intersection, field would have two directions.
→ **Both true; R explains A.**

---

**Q54.** Assertion: Potential energy of dipole is minimum when aligned.
Reason: Stable equilibrium corresponds to minimum PE.
→ **Both true; R explains A.**

---

**Q55.** Assertion: CO₂ has zero dipole moment.
Reason: It has linear structure.
→ **Both true; R explains A.**
