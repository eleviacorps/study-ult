## SECTION D: ASSERTION-REASON QUESTIONS

**Q91.** Assertion: Electric field inside a uniformly charged spherical shell is zero.
Reason: All field lines emanating from charges on the shell terminate on other charges on the shell itself.

**Q92.** Assertion: Potential energy of a dipole in uniform electric field is minimum when it is aligned with the field.
Reason: Stable equilibrium corresponds to minimum potential energy.

**Q93.** Assertion: Two field lines can never cross each other.
Reason: At point of intersection, electric field would have two different directions simultaneously.

**Q94.** Assertion: Coulomb's force is much stronger than gravitational force between two protons.
Reason: Gravitational force is inversely proportional to square of distance, while Coulomb's force is directly proportional to product of charges.

**Q95.** Assertion: Electric flux through a closed surface is zero if no charge is enclosed.
Reason: Gauss's law states flux equals charge enclosed divided by ε₀.

**Q96.** Assertion: A charge placed at the center of a cube experiences no net force from charges outside the cube.
Reason: By symmetry, forces from opposite faces cancel.

**Q97.** Assertion: The dipole moment of NH₃ molecule is non-zero even without external field.
Reason: NH₃ has trigonal pyramidal structure with N at apex.

**Q98.** Assertion: Electric field is zero everywhere inside a conductor in electrostatic equilibrium.
Reason: Any field would cause charge carriers to move, violating equilibrium condition.

**Q99.** Assertion: The force on a charge outside a uniformly charged sphere is same as if all charge were at center.
Reason: This follows from Newton's shell theorem, valid for inverse-square forces.

**Q100.** Assertion: Quantization of charge is not observable at macroscopic scale.
Reason: The elementary charge e is extremely small compared to typical macroscopic charges.

## ANSWER KEY

### Section A Answers

1. When spheres touch, charges redistribute equally → each gets (5+3)/2 = 4 μC
2. Electrons transfer from glass to silk (glass becomes +)
3. Quantization (q = ne), Additivity (algebraic sum), Conservation (total constant)
4. Initially neutral; after combing, comb acquires charge which polarizes paper
5. q = ne where n is integer; e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C is too small to detect at macro level
6. F = kq(Q-q)/r²; dF/dq = 0 → q = Q/2
7. No, because at intersection point field would have two directions
8. (a) Radially outward, (b) Radially inward
9. p = q×2a; direction from -q to +q; SI unit: C·m
10. Non-zero; U = kq₁q₂/r
11. Yes, if field is zero. Equilibrium is unstable (saddle point in general)
12. +Q + (-Q) = 0, split equally → each has 0
13. Flux through closed surface = q/ε₀; Yes, holds for any charge distribution
14. Φ = E·S cosθ; Scalar (dot product)
15. By Gauss's law, no charge enclosed inside shell
