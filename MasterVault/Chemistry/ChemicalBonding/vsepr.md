# VSEPR

VSEPR predicts shape by minimizing repulsion between electron domains around a central atom. It links to [[Chemical Bonding Core]] and [[Hybridisation]].

Repulsion order:

Lone pair - lone pair > lone pair - bond pair > bond pair - bond pair.

- Q:: Why is water bent instead of linear? A:: Oxygen has two lone pairs, and lone-pair repulsion compresses the H-O-H angle.
- MCQ:: Electron geometry for four electron domains is: Options:: Tetrahedral | Linear | Octahedral | Trigonal planar Answer:: Tetrahedral
